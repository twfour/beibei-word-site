Read the story in easier English

Many people once told arts and humanities students to learn coding if they wanted good jobs. The article says this advice may now look less certain. As AI becomes stronger, many programmers are worried that machines may take their work. At the same time, big AI companies are hiring many philosophers. Philosophy can help AI researchers because AI creates difficult questions. One example is the Socratic method. It uses careful questions to find problems in an idea. AI models trained in this way may be less eager to please people and more willing to search for the truth. Another useful idea is humility. Socrates said that he was wise because he knew he did not know everything. If AI models can learn this kind of humility, they may make fewer false claims and become less overconfident. Philosophy is also important for AI safety. Developers need rules to stop models from behaving badly. Some rules come from deontology, which says some actions are always wrong. Other systems use consequentialism, which compares costs and benefits. The article ends with a warning. If machines make more moral decisions for us, people may become less able to make their own judgments. In the age of AI, philosophers may have more work than ever.

SENTENCE 1
The Socratic method—as described by Plato, an ancient Greek philosopher—uses feigned ignorance and sequential questioning to clarify meanings, spot contradictions and reveal ramifications.

1. 句子主干（核心主谓宾） The Socratic method uses feigned ignorance and sequential questioning 主语：The Socratic method（苏格拉底诘问法，单数名词短语） 谓语：uses（一般现在时，三单动词） 宾语：feigned ignorance and sequential questioning（并列两个名词短语作宾语） feigned ignorance：佯装无知（feigned 过去分词作定语修饰 ignorance） sequential questioning：层层递进式追问（sequential 形容词定语）

2. 插入分隔语：—as described by Plato, an ancient Greek philosopher— 两个破折号之间是后置省略式定语从句，用来补充解释主语 The Socratic method： 完整还原 = as it is described by Plato as：关系连词，引导方式定语从句，表 “正如、如同” described：过去分词，被动语态

-

by Plato：动作发出者 an ancient Greek philosopher：同位语，就近修饰 Plato，解释柏拉图身份，相当于非限定定语从句 who was an ancient Greek philosopher 作用：打断主句，补充说明这套方法出自柏拉图笔下，去掉不影响主句完整句意。

3. 目的状语：to clarify meanings, spot contradictions and reveal ramifications 不定式短语整体作 uses...to do sth 的目的状语，表 “使用两种手段是为了达成三件事”。 这里是三个并列不带 to 的不定式（共用前面的 to）： (to) clarify meanings 厘清概念含义 (to) spot contradictions 发现逻辑矛盾 (to) reveal ramifications 推演连锁后果 并列结构：A, B and C

Popular with Kant, among others, this imposes strict rules that prohibit things like lying, coercion and treating people as a means rather than an end, even if it is for a greater good.

1. 句首形容词短语：Popular with Kant, among others 形容词短语作插入语（前置定语），对主语 this（即义务论）进行补充说明。

3. 定语从句 that prohibit things... that 引导限定性定语从句，修饰先行词 strict rules 从句主干：that prohibit things 从句内部结构 like lying, coercion and treating people as a means rather than an end 介词 like 引出三类被禁止的事物，三者并列： lying (动名词) 说谎 coercion (名词) 胁迫 treating people as a means rather than an end (动名词短语) 核心：treating people 宾补：as a means 对比状语：rather than an end
